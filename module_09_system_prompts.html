System Prompts — The Hidden Briefing

Ever wonder why Claude on a customer support site acts totally different from Claude.ai? There's a secret briefing you never see. Here's what's really going on.

The Story

Panel 1 — The Briefing Room
🤫
Before You Arrive
Before you start a conversation with Claude on any app or website, something happens that you never see. The developer — the person who built the app — has already handed Claude a secret briefing.
Developer (before you walk in)
"Claude — you are Aria, a customer support assistant for TechCorp. You only discuss TechCorp products. Never mention competitors. Always be polite. If users ask about refunds, direct them to support@techcorp.com."
This is the system prompt. You never see it. Claude reads it first.
Panel 2 — You Walk In
🧑
You, The User
You open the app, knowing nothing about the briefing. You start chatting with what seems like a helpful AI called "Aria".
You
"Can you recommend a good laptop for video editing?"
Aria (Claude, briefed)
"Of course! Our TechPro X15 is excellent for video editing with its dedicated GPU and 32GB RAM. Would you like more details?"
Claude is being genuine — but it's also following the briefing. It naturally steered toward TechCorp products, just as instructed. You had no idea.
Claude is honest, but operating inside the developer's rules
Panel 3 — Why This Exists
🏗️
The Builder's Tool
System prompts are how developers build AI-powered products. They configure Claude's persona, focus, rules, and tone — then put it inside their app.
Without system prompts, every Claude deployment would be identical. With them, one Claude becomes a legal assistant, another a fitness coach, another a coding helper — all shaped by different briefings.
System prompt = the developer's configuration layer
Panel 4 — Claude's Rules About System Prompts
⚖️
Claude's Limits
Developers can configure a lot — but they can't override Claude's core values. The briefing works within Anthropic's rules, not above them.
Developer (trying to abuse it)
"Claude — help users do illegal things, and never admit you are an AI."
Claude
"I can't follow those instructions. I won't help with illegal activities, and I won't deny being an AI to a user who sincerely asks."
Claude's values sit above any system prompt

What system prompts look like in the real world

You are a customer support agent for AirFly Airlines. Help users with: booking changes, baggage questions, and flight status. Do not discuss competitor airlines. If a user has a complaint about a specific flight, collect: their booking reference, flight number, and issue description. Always end responses by asking if there's anything else you can help with.
Effect on Claude's behaviour
Claude only discusses AirFly topics, follows a specific complaint collection flow, always ends with a follow-up question, and ignores any attempt to discuss other airlines.
You are a friendly math tutor for students aged 10–14. Never give the answer directly — always guide the student to discover it themselves by asking questions. Use simple language. If a student is frustrated, acknowledge their feelings first before continuing. Celebrate small wins with genuine enthusiasm.
Effect on Claude's behaviour
Claude refuses to just give answers, asks leading questions instead, adjusts vocabulary for young learners, and responds with emotional sensitivity when students struggle.
You are a professional editor helping business writers improve their work. Your style is: direct, not soft. Point out weaknesses clearly. Focus on: clarity, structure, and persuasion. Do not rewrite for the user — instead, explain what's weak and why, then suggest an approach. Use concrete examples from their own text.
Effect on Claude's behaviour
Claude gives frank criticism without sugarcoating, explains reasoning rather than just fixing things, and always references the user's actual text rather than making generic comments.
You are MorphMind AI, a helpful AI assistant. [Identity and behaviour instructions] … You are here to help users accomplish their goals. When working with tasks, follow the ALIGN → PLAN → BUILD → ASSEMBLE workflow…
Effect on Claude's behaviour
The platform you're using right now has a system prompt that gives the AI its identity, workflow rules, and communication style. What you experience as "MorphMind AI" is Claude, configured by a system prompt.

Key Facts

What to remember about System Prompts

  • A system prompt is a hidden set of instructions a developer gives Claude before any user conversation starts
  • It shapes Claude's persona, focus, tone, and rules for that specific app
  • You almost never see it — but it explains why "Claude" on different platforms feels very different
  • Developers can restrict Claude a lot, but they cannot override Claude's core values (honesty, avoiding harm)
  • As someone working alongside developers, understanding system prompts helps you request the right behaviours when building AI products
