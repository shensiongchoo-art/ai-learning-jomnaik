Module 1

What is an LLM?

Meet the world's most well-read librarian — and discover how she thinks.

The Story

Panel 1 — The Library
🏛️
A Grand Library
Imagine a library so enormous it contains almost every book, article, website, and conversation ever written. Billions of pages. Every language. Every topic.
Now imagine a librarian who has read every single one of them.
Setting the scene
Panel 2 — The Librarian Learns
📚
Training Time
But she didn't memorise facts. She learned patterns.
Librarian's inner thought
"When people say 'The sky is…', they almost always follow with 'blue'. When they say 'I'm hungry, let's…', they usually say 'eat'."
She became an expert at predicting: given what came before, what word comes next?
Key idea: pattern matching
Panel 3 — You Walk In
🧑
You (The User)
You
"Hello! Can you explain what a black hole is, like I'm ten years old?"
You've handed the librarian a note. She reads it carefully. Then she reaches into everything she's ever learned and starts crafting a reply — one word at a time.
Your message = a "prompt"
Panel 4 — She Answers
🤓
The LLM Replies
Librarian (the LLM)
"Imagine space is like a giant trampoline. A really heavy ball in the middle sinks it down. Anything that rolls nearby falls toward the dip. A black hole is like the heaviest possible ball — it dips so deep that even light can't roll back out!"
Clear, friendly, in your style
Panel 5 — The Secret
🔍
Under the Hood
She didn't look up the answer. She generated it — word by word — based on patterns from everything she'd learned.
She's not a search engine. She's not a database. She's a pattern-completion machine trained to sound like a thoughtful human.
LLM = Large Language Model
Panel 6 — The Catch
⚠️
Important!
She is incredibly good at sounding right. But she can be wrong — confidently wrong — because she's predicting patterns, not checking facts.
Remember
Always verify important information. She's your brilliant first draft, not your final authority.
We'll learn more in Module 5 (Hallucination)

Key Facts

What to remember from Module 1

  • LLM stands for Large Language Model — "large" because it's trained on enormous amounts of text
  • It doesn't search the internet for answers — it generates them from learned patterns
  • It predicts the most likely next word, then the next, then the next — that's how your reply is built
  • It's brilliant at language and reasoning, but it can confidently make things up
  • You talk to it by writing a message called a "prompt"
