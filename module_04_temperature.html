Temperature — Dial Up the Creativity

Why does the same question sometimes get a boring answer and sometimes a brilliant one? Meet Temperature.

The Analogy

Panel 1 — The Two Chefs
👨‍🍳
Two Chefs, Same Kitchen
Imagine two chefs given the same task: "Make me something with chicken."
Chef Predictable (Low Temperature):
"Certainly. I'll make roast chicken with potatoes and gravy. Classic, reliable, exactly what you'd expect."
Chef Creative (High Temperature):
"Ooh! Spicy mango chicken tacos with a lime-coconut drizzle and candied chilli flakes on top — trust me!"
Low temp = safe & predictable High temp = creative & surprising
Panel 2 — The Dial
🎛️
The Temperature Setting
Temperature is a number, usually between 0 and 1 (sometimes up to 2).
At temperature = 0, the LLM always picks the most likely next word. Completely predictable.
At temperature = 1+, the LLM picks more randomly — it rolls the dice and chooses less expected words. More surprising, more creative, but also more likely to go off-track.
Neither is better — it depends what you need

Key Facts

What to remember about Temperature

  • Temperature controls how predictable vs. creative the LLM's output is
  • Low temperature = picks the safest, most expected word every time
  • High temperature = takes more risks, picks surprising words — great for creativity, risky for facts
  • Most LLM apps set a default temperature automatically — you often don't need to touch it
  • If the LLM gives boring, repetitive answers → try higher temperature. If it goes off-topic → lower it
