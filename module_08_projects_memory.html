Claude Projects & Memory

Why does Claude forget you every time you start a new chat? And how do Projects fix that? Meet your AI's permanent desk.

The Story

Panel 1 — The Freelance Contractor
👷
Claude as Contractor
Imagine you hire a brilliant freelance contractor — smart, capable, hardworking. But here's the catch: every morning they show up having forgotten everything from yesterday. You have to re-introduce yourself. Re-explain the project. Re-share all the files.
That's what it's like using Claude without Projects. Each new conversation = a brand new contractor who knows nothing about you.
New chat = blank slate, every time
Panel 2 — The Permanent Desk
🗂️
Claude Projects
Claude Projects gives your contractor a permanent desk in your office. On that desk, you put everything they need to know: your company background, how you like to work, key documents, your style guidelines.
Now, every morning when they arrive, they sit at their desk, read their notes — and they're ready to go. No re-introduction needed.
Project = a briefed, context-aware workspace
Panel 3 — Before Projects vs. After
💬
Same User, Two Worlds
Without Projects — every chat
"Hi! I'm Sarah, I run a small bakery called Sweet Crumbs in Penang. I need help writing an Instagram caption about our new mango cake. Oh, our tone is friendly and a bit playful, we don't use formal language…"
With a Project — every chat
"Hey! What are we writing today?" (It already knows Sarah, Sweet Crumbs, the tone, the audience, and the brand.)
Context carries forward — you skip the re-introduction every time
Panel 4 — What You Put On the Desk
📋
Project Instructions
When you create a Claude Project, you write Project Instructions — a briefing that Claude reads at the start of every conversation in that project.
Example Project Instructions
"You are helping me run my bakery business 'Sweet Crumbs'. We are based in Penang, Malaysia. Our audience is young adults 20–35. Our brand tone is warm, playful, and never overly formal. We never use the word 'delicious'. Our speciality is fusion Asian-Western pastries."
Claude reads this before every conversation in the project

What You Can Put in a Project

Your permanent desk can hold:

📝
Instructions
Who you are, what the project is for, tone preferences, things Claude should always or never do
📄
Documents
Upload PDFs, text files, spreadsheets — Claude reads them and can reference them in any chat
🎨
Style Guides
Your brand voice, writing samples, formatting rules — Claude learns your style
💬
Conversation History
Past chats within the project are visible — Claude can reference what you discussed before

What Persists vs. What Resets

Understanding Project memory boundaries

Stays in the Project ✓

  • Project instructions you wrote
  • Files you uploaded to the project
  • Conversations within the project
  • Claude's "awareness" of your context

Resets Each New Chat ↺

  • The working memory of the current conversation (context window)
  • Anything you said in a different project
  • Chats outside any project
  • Claude's "opinions" — it doesn't build opinions over time

Key Facts

What to remember about Claude Projects

  • By default, Claude remembers nothing between separate conversations
  • Claude Projects give Claude a permanent briefing — your context loads automatically every chat
  • Put your instructions, documents, and style guides in the project — Claude reads them all
  • Projects are for ongoing work — great for business, writing, research, or any repeated task
  • The context window (from Module 3) still applies inside a project — but the project instructions always reload fresh
